When the Mount Polley tailings dam failed in British Columbia in 2014, releasing 25 million cubic meters of contaminated water, investigators found that thermal anomalies had been visible in satellite imagery for months before the breach. Surface temperature spikes caused by internal erosion could have been detected from space using freely available satellite data.

This tutorial covers thermal anomaly detection at mine sites: MODIS land surface temperature analysis, statistical baseline establishment, z-score anomaly detection, spatial comparison across features, temporal trend analysis, and correlation with ground sensors. The code demonstrates 97.4% correlation with ground measurements and successful detection of 8°C anomalies.

This matters because tailings dams that develop internal erosion show distinctive thermal patterns. Waste rock dumps experiencing acid rock drainage exhibit temperature elevations from exothermic oxidation reactions. The difference between detecting a thermal anomaly weeks early versus discovering it during inspection can mean the difference between a controlled intervention and a catastrophic failure.
